The Good News

Jesus said, “I am the bread of life” (John 6:35). God made you for Himself. Our sin separates us from Him, but Jesus died and rose again so that all who trust Him are forgiven and have eternal life.

  • God loves you (Genesis 1:27)
  • Sin separates us (Romans 3:23)
  • Jesus saves (1 Cor 15:3–4)
  • Respond in faith (Romans 10:9–10)
“Jesus said to him, I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me.” — John 14:6

How to Be Saved

Salvation is a gift from God through Jesus Christ. Here’s how you can respond:

  1. Believe — Trust that Jesus died for your sins and rose again (John 3:16).
  2. Confess — Acknowledge your sins before God and ask for forgiveness (Romans 10:9).
  3. Repent — Turn from sin and choose to follow Christ (Acts 3:19).
  4. Receive — Invite Jesus into your life as Lord and Savior (Revelation 3:20).
“If you declare with your mouth, ‘Jesus is Lord,’ and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved.” — Romans 10:9

You can pray: “Jesus, I believe You died for my sins and rose again. Forgive me, make me new, and help me follow You. Amen.”

Next Steps

Have you decided to follow Jesus or want to grow deeper? Here are some practical next steps:

1. Pray

Talk with God daily. Share your heart and listen to His Word.

2. Read the Bible

Begin with the Gospel of John or Mark. Read a little each day.

3. Find Community

Join a Bible-believing church or small group for support and growth. Find a church near you →

4. Be Baptized

Take the step of baptism as a public declaration of your faith.

Welcoming Blessings: How Scripture Inspires Us to Bless Those Who Pass By

BreadWay.org exists to share the Good News of Jesus through a simple, familiar symbol: the bread. Whether displayed on a car, home, storefront, or church, our signs and stickers serve as silent messengers, offering blessings, inviting reflection, and pointing others to Christ.

Each product is more than just a sign. It is a prayer in motion and a spark for conversation. Every item purchased helps us continue creating and distributing these messages of hope, so that more hearts can be reached and more lives can be touched.

The Bread and the Way: Why We Call It BreadWay.org

“Jesus said to him, ‘I am the way, and the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through me.’” — John 14:6
“I am the bread of life. Whoever comes to me will never go hungry, and whoever believes in me will never be thirsty.” — John 6:35

The BreadWay is our name for this powerful, symbolic path where Jesus is both the Bread of Life and the Way to the Father. Bread is universal, simple, nourishing, and ever-present. Jesus used it to describe Himself because it was something people understood. Our use of the bread symbol is intentional. It draws from daily life to convey eternal truth. Just as bread feeds the body, Jesus feeds the soul.

When someone sees the bread symbol, it can stir questions, open hearts, and guide conversations toward the life-saving message of Christ.

Why Bless Those Who Pass?

“The Lord bless you and keep you; the Lord make His face shine upon you and be gracious to you; the Lord lift up His countenance upon you and give you peace.” — Numbers 6:24–26

When you place a sign in your yard or a sticker on your car that says, “Bless all who pass this house” or “Bless all who pass this car,” you are speaking life and peace over your neighbors, community, and every traveler who crosses your path.

These are not just decorative words. They are active blessings, rooted in Scripture and drawn from a rich biblical tradition of showing kindness to others.

The Scriptural Foundation of Blessing Others

Luke 10:25–37 (The Good Samaritan)
The Samaritan showed mercy to a stranger in need. Like him, we are called to extend compassion in everyday encounters. A sticker or sign can be your small act of mercy, blessing someone you may never meet.

Hebrews 13:2
“Do not forget to show hospitality to strangers, for by so doing some people have entertained angels without knowing it.” Every person who passes is worthy of kindness. Our signs are daily reminders, for you and for those who see them, to live with open hearts and open hands.

Psalm 121:8
“The Lord will watch over your coming and going both now and forevermore.” As people pass your home or vehicle, this blessing becomes a quiet prayer. It asks that God would protect, guide, and keep them in all their travels.

Isaiah 30:21
“Whether you turn to the right or to the left, your ears will hear a voice behind you, saying, ‘This is the way; walk in it.’” In a world full of uncertainty, these signs are a soft encouragement that divine guidance is near, for those in transition, in doubt, or on any kind of journey.

Psalm 121:3
“He will not let your foot slip—He who watches over you will not slumber.” Our message extends this blessing to all who pass. May your steps be steady, your footing secure, and your path aligned with peace.

A Quiet Witness, A Bold Message

A sign on your door. A sticker on your rear window. A message in your business or ministry space. These are not loud declarations. They are sacred whispers, quietly covering your space in prayer and blessing everyone who sees them.
